How Do You Build a PC?

Components

We will be talking about how to build a PC including the parts you will need
and links to a few guides. We recommend that when making a list of components
that you go to or the PcPartPicker as it will tell you whether the parts
you have chosen are compatible with eachother as well as display the best prices
for the components that you have chosen to use.

Motherbaord

The motherboard is the circuit board where everything comes together.
Once everything comes together it is a completely functional computer.
It comes in many sizes which most commonly are: ATX,microAtx, or ITX.
Depending if you go with Intel or AMD you will have to get the specific model that supports their CPUs
Motherboards usually come in different tiers and bring different features. For Example,
X570 and B550 motherboards have Pcie Gen 4 support meanwhile all boards that support intel don't.
Also if you are planning to overclock you may want to look into a motherboard with a great VRM Setup.

CPU

The CPU is the brain of your computer and also the most important.
This is one of the expensive parts but depending on what you're planning to
with the computer you are building it might not be the most expensive.
do with this part your two options are Intel CPUs or AMD CPUs. The Advantage with Intel
is their Single core performance and higher clock speeds so if you are focused more
on playing games then Intel is way to go. If you are rendering videos or are using tasks that
require many threads like Live streaming while gaming then AMD is the way to go as their CPUs
have all around great performance and more cores.

CPU Cooler

The CPU coolers are responsible for keeping you CPU cool or at moderate temperatures.
If you are planning to overclock then you may want to look into making a custom water loop
or an AIO cooler. Of course you can also use something like the Noctua NH-U12s or the Noctua D15 .
Ig you aren't planning on overclocking then you will be fine with the stock cooler that comes with your
Ryzen CPU but if you got Intel then the Hyper 212 EVO by cooler master is recommended

Graphics Card

The Graphics Card is the most important component depending on your usage.
If you are planning to play games, edit videos, or doing other graphical intensive
tasks then you will need to buy one according to what you need. At the moment NVIDIA
is leading in terms of the high end graphics card with their RTX 2080 TI graphics card.
For the mid-range you might want to look into AMD's Radeon RX 5700 XT graphics card.
For anybody on more of a budget then they should consider one of many NVIDIA's budget options
Like the GTX 1060 Super or the GTX 1050 Ti

Ram

RAM is very important as it stores cache for programs to call in a very short amount of time.
If you are planning to game, livestream, or edit videos then you should look into getting a 16GB RAM Kit.
If you are planning on just using the computer for web surfing then 8GB is recommended.
On Intel's side we do recommend we recommend the Corsair's Vengeance LPX 16 GB kit
and on AMD's side we recommend GSKill's Trident Z Royal 16 GB kit.

Power Supply

The Power Supply you need is very dependant on your components and their power consumption.
On this part you shouldn't cheap out on because this one could potentially break a component
if it isn't certified. For this one we do recommend EVGA's 750W 80 PLUS GOLD Modular Power Supply

Storage

For the storage we recommend you go with something faster like an SSD then later down
the road you get a high capacity hdd with it to store other less important files.
The SSD will make it so that the PC boots into the OS faster than something like a hdd.
For this we recommend Samsung's 500GB 970 EVO PLus m.2 drive and
with that you can get a hhd, specifically Seagate's BarraCuda 2TB 7200 RPM hard drive

Case

For this case we can't recommend anything as it's the most customaizable part
There are so many options in the market but one thing to be aware of is to make sure
that the case you are buying is compatible with your Motherboard and check if the case
has the kind of performance you are looking for as it is closed afterall.

OS

For OS there are really only three options: Windows, MacOS, or Linux
Linux is open source and many prefer it for many reasons such as concern for privacy.
Windows 10 is also another very popular option as it is very user friendly the only concern
being is that you are more vulnerable to Viruses as there is a larger install base and that
Microsoft may collect your data. As for MacOS it is a bit more complicated to install into a PC
that you built but it is possible with Virtualization.
